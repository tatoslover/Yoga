{% if target == "standing" %}

Standing poses form the foundation of yoga practice. They build strength, improve balance, and enhance focus.

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

The foundation of all standing poses, teaching proper alignment and stability.

Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

A powerful standing pose that builds strength and stamina while opening the chest.

Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

An elegant pose that stretches and strengthens the entire body while improving balance.

{% elif target == "seated" %}

Seated poses improve flexibility, especially in the hips and spine, and cultivate a sense of groundedness.

Easy Pose (Sukhasana)

A simple cross-legged position that provides a stable base for breathing and meditation.

Staff Pose (Dandasana)

The foundation for all seated forward bends, teaching proper spinal alignment.

Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)

A deep forward bend that stretches the entire back of the body from heels to head.

{% elif target == "backbends" %}

Backbends open the chest and shoulders, increase spinal mobility, and counteract the effects of sitting.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

A gentle backbend that strengthens the spine and opens the chest and shoulders.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

A gentle backbend that strengthens the back, glutes, and hamstrings while opening the chest.

Camel Pose (Ustrasana)

A deep backbend that opens the entire front of the body while strengthening the back.

{% elif target == "inversions" %}

Inversions reverse the effects of gravity, bringing fresh blood flow to the brain and offering a new perspective.

Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

A mild inversion that creates length in the spine while strengthening the arms and legs.

Legs-Up-The-Wall (Viparita Karani)

A gentle, restorative inversion that relieves tired legs and calms the nervous system.

Shoulder Stand (Sarvangasana)

Often called the "queen of asanas," this inversion brings numerous benefits to the entire body.

{% endif %}
