{% if target == "beginners" %}

Start Where You Are

Honor your body's current abilities and limitations. Choose classes that challenge you appropriately without causing strain or injury.

Be Consistent

Regular practice, even if brief, is more beneficial than occasional long sessions. Aim for consistency rather than intensity.

Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to how different practices make you feel. Your body will tell you what it needs—sometimes gentle restoration, other times more vigorous movement.

{% elif target == "mindful" %}

Breathe Mindfully

The breath is at the core of yoga practice. Focus on deep, steady breathing to enhance the benefits of your practice and stay present.

Practice With Intention

Set an intention at the beginning of each practice. This could be a quality you want to cultivate, a dedication, or simply an area of focus.

Stay Present

When your mind wanders (and it will), gently bring your attention back to your breath and the sensations in your body.

{% elif target == "props" %}

Use Props When Needed

Yoga props like blocks, straps, and bolsters are not just for beginners—they're tools that can help practitioners of all levels access poses safely.

Create a Dedicated Space

Designate a specific area for your practice, even if it's just enough room for your mat. Having a consistent space helps establish a routine.

Comfortable Clothing

Wear clothes that allow free movement without constant adjustment. You want to focus on your practice, not your outfit.
