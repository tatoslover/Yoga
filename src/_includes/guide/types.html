{% if type == "Vinyasa" %}

Overview

Vinyasa yoga is a dynamic practice that synchronizes breath with movement. The term "Vinyasa" translates to "to place in a special way," referring to the careful sequencing of poses that flow one into the next.

Philosophy

Vinyasa emphasizes the connection between breath and movement, creating a moving meditation that builds internal heat and improves cardiovascular health.

Benefits

Improves strength, flexibility, and balance while calming the mind. The continuous movement helps build endurance and the breath focus enhances mental clarity.

Who It's For

Great for practitioners who enjoy a more athletic practice, those looking to build heat in the body, and anyone who appreciates creative sequencing and variety.

{% elif type == "Hatha" %}

Overview

Hatha yoga is a traditional form that emphasizes holding postures for longer periods while focusing on alignment and breath. "Ha" represents sun energy and "tha" represents moon energy, symbolizing balance.

Philosophy

Hatha focuses on balancing opposing forces and energies in the body. It's considered the foundation for many modern yoga styles and emphasizes the physical aspects of yoga practice.

Benefits

Improves flexibility, strength, and posture while reducing stress. The slower pace allows for deeper exploration of poses and greater attention to proper alignment.

Who It's For

Perfect for beginners learning the fundamentals, those recovering from injury, or practitioners seeking a more mindful, slower-paced practice with attention to detail.

{% elif type == "Yin" %}

Overview

Yin yoga is a slow-paced style where poses are held for extended periods, typically 3-5 minutes or longer. It targets the deep connective tissues of the body rather than the muscles.

Philosophy

Yin is based on Taoist concepts and targets the body's meridian system to improve energy flow. It embraces the concept of surrender and working with, rather than against, limitation.

Benefits

Increases flexibility in joints and connective tissues, reduces stress, and improves joint mobility. The meditative approach promotes mindfulness and emotional release.

Who It's For

Great for athletes needing recovery, those with tight muscles from repetitive movements, and anyone seeking a more meditative, introspective practice to balance an active lifestyle.

{% elif type == "Power" %}

Overview

Power yoga is a fitness-based approach to vinyasa-style yoga. It builds strength, flexibility, and stamina through a dynamic, flowing practice that challenges the body and focuses the mind.

Philosophy

Power yoga was developed in the 1990s as a more accessible, fitness-oriented version of Ashtanga yoga. It emphasizes building core strength and creating internal heat.

Benefits

Builds muscular strength and endurance, increases calorie burn, enhances cardiovascular health, and promotes mental fortitude through challenging sequences.

Who It's For

Ideal for fitness enthusiasts, those looking for a more physical challenge, practitioners wanting to build strength, and anyone seeking a vigorous, sweat-inducing practice.

{% elif type == "Restorative" %}

Overview

Restorative yoga is a gentle, therapeutic style that uses props to support the body in positions of comfort and ease. Poses are typically held for 5-10 minutes to facilitate deep relaxation.

Philosophy

Restorative yoga is based on the principle that deep relaxation is essential for healing. It activates the parasympathetic nervous system, allowing the body to rest, digest, and repair.

Benefits

Reduces stress and anxiety, improves sleep quality, lowers blood pressure, and supports recovery from illness or injury. It can help manage chronic conditions by activating the body's relaxation response.

Who It's For

Perfect for those recovering from illness or injury, dealing with chronic stress or anxiety, experiencing burnout, or anyone needing deep rest and nervous system regulation.
